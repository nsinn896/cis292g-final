This was made in Photoshop for a graphic design project.
I'm fond of simplistic imagery, and for pushing an urgent message, the style works wonders.
I made the hospital graphic myself, and the QR code is functional as well.
It redirects you to Google and searches for hospitals near you.

This was one of my first Illustrator projects. I was tasked with re-making a pre-existing album cover.
I chose to remake the "Scaled and Icy" cover. It's an album from my favorite band, Twenty One Pilots,
whom I listen to a lot whilst I work on my pieces.

I made this in Photoshop for a class a few years back. I forgot what the assignment was, but this was labeled "August".
The cat is a character I made and draw a lot; I tend to use it to express myself and the emotions I internalize.
The cat is nearly, if not, a ten year old creation at this point that I'm very proud of.

This was a relatively recent project made in Photoshop for a class.
Inspired by Sleeping Beauty, I donned one of my old dance costumes, set up the scene myself, then took my picture.
I then took the image to Photoshop and went wild with effects. I didn't expect it to come out as well as it did, but I'm proud of it.

This was the May 2024 Heron cover. I placed the borders and text using InDesign.
At first, I had drop shadows and more gradients, but for printing reasons, those had to be removed.
Regardless, I'm happy with how it came out! It's so strange seeing its picture pop up here and there knowing I assembled it.

I don't remember when I made this, just like the August project. This was also made in Photoshop for the same assignment.
I chose this one as well to display because I admire how dedicated I was to truly immersing my character into each image,
going so far as to add a sense of perspective into the mix.

Made with my father's camera and the power of Photoshop,
this has to be one of my favorite photography products I've made. I always love leaving imagery such as this up to audience interpretation,
as it can be so much more impactful that way. I think that aspect of this piece is what truly makes it special to me.
